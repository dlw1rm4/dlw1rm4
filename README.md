## About Me

I'm in my 2nd year of college at UC San Diego, studying computer science. Originally, I pursued coding because I was bad at it, since I kept writing code that didn't work in ROBLOX Studio. I ended up staying because I enjoyed creating projects that myself and others could use. As of writing this, I'm in [Women in Computing (WIC)](https://ucsdwic.github.io/) as the Tech Dev: Project Teams Chair Intern. I enjoy being a part of the UCSD WIC community, so I'd appreciate it if you read more about my experience in the club through this [link](#women-in-computing).

People close to me have told me that I'm creative, reliable, and ambitious. I also get that I have a lot of **initiative**, *especially* when it comes to working on team projects.

### The Story of my Last Name 
My last name is unique because it looks like a common Vietnamese last name (Nguyen) without 'en', but I'm actually fully Chinese. Unfortunately, I'm only fluent when my mom is yelling at me in Cantonese!

According to my mom, my family's surname was Wei (魏). However, my dad's family (and also my mom's) fled from China during the Cultural Revolution to Vietnam, where their last name was translated into Vietnamese. 

### Women in Computing
Although Women in Computing is a UC San Diego organization, I was actually in it in my senior year of high school and was a part of their high school mentorship program (EDGE). As an EDGE mentee, I won Best Mentee Representative of 2023-2024, which is given to only one of over 100 participants in the program. My time in the program helped me find confidence in myself as an aspiring female software engineer, as well as taught me how valuable it is to empower and foster curiosity in underrepresented groups. 

The EDGE program and Women in Computing @ UC San Diego had so much of an impact in my love for computing that I actually chose UC San Diego CS over UC Berkeley EECS (and I wouldn't have changed a thing). I originally wanted to lead the EDGE program to give back what I got out of the program, but I ended up loving project teams a lot more. It's really nice being able to be a part of someone's learning experience, whether they are a beginner or a veteran, while giving them the space to be comfortable enough to create something they're proud of.

Being a part of this organization taught me not only the importance of women, but also the importance of every single individual voice. There's so much more I can say about WIC, but it would make this page way longer than it should be. Feel free to contact me to talk about this!

### 3D Modeling
During the COVID-19 pandemic, I got into 3D modeling on Blender. For a little over a year, I was creating models for at least 3 hours a day, hoping that people would enjoy and use my work. Luckily, a few video game studios liked my work enough to commission me. However, I reached a larger audience on YouTube (which I will not share out of privacy reasons), where I gained nearly **2,000** subscribers by teaching people how to 3D model in Blender. Although I no longer create videos, I still try to respond to comments when I can.
